## User-authored summary

Topical NSAIDs (such as topical diclofenac) are a guideline-supported treatment option
for knee osteoarthritis and are generally better tolerated than oral NSAIDs. Evidence
indicates topical and oral NSAIDs provide broadly similar pain relief for knee OA,
while topical formulations are associated with fewer systemic adverse effects —
notably less gastrointestinal harm — because of lower systemic absorption. OARSI
guidance strongly recommends topical NSAIDs while only conditionally recommending oral
NSAIDs. Topical NSAIDs are described as a particularly appropriate first-line
consideration for older patients and those with comorbidities or risk factors for
gastrointestinal, renal, or cardiovascular adverse events from oral NSAIDs.
