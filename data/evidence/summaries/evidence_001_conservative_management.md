## User-authored summary

This guideline addresses non-surgical (non-arthroplasty) management of knee
osteoarthritis in adults. It gives a strong recommendation for exercise
(supervised, unsupervised, or aquatic) over no exercise to improve pain and
function. It recommends sustained weight loss to improve pain and function in
patients who are overweight or obese. It supports topical NSAIDs, and addresses
oral NSAIDs and other options, for symptom relief where appropriate. The
guideline focuses on conservative management and does not by itself determine
which patients should proceed to surgery.

## Useful for agent

- Identifying first-line, non-surgical treatment options.
- Comparing conservative care against orthopedic/surgical evaluation.
- Explaining that treatment choice depends on severity, prior response,
  patient goals, and clinician judgment.

## Claims allowed from this source

- Exercise is recommended over no exercise to improve pain and function in
  knee osteoarthritis.
- Sustained weight loss is recommended to improve pain and function in
  overweight or obese patients with knee osteoarthritis.
- Topical NSAIDs may be used to improve function and quality of life when not
  contraindicated.
