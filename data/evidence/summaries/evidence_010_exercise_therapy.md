## User-authored summary

Exercise is a cornerstone of knee osteoarthritis management and is recommended by all
current international clinical guidelines, regardless of age, comorbidity, or pain
severity. Exercise programs studied are heterogeneous — including muscle strengthening,
aerobic, range-of-motion, balance, stretching, and mixed programs — delivered
individually or in groups, supervised or unsupervised, on land or in water. Land-based
and aquatic programs both show positive effects. Guidance commonly encourages regular
aerobic, muscle-strengthening, and range-of-motion exercise, often with referral to a
physical therapist for assessment and instruction. An important honesty note: the 2024
Cochrane update found that most trials in this area were at unclear or high risk of
bias, so while the direction of benefit is consistent and guideline-endorsed, the
strength of the underlying evidence has real methodological limitations.

## Useful for agent

- Giving conservative management concrete content (what kind of exercise, not just "exercise").
- Explaining that both land-based and aquatic programs are supported.
- Demonstrating honest reporting of evidence quality, not just direction of effect.

## Claims allowed from this source

- Exercise is recommended by current international guidelines as a core treatment for
  knee osteoarthritis, regardless of age, comorbidity, or pain severity.
- Supported exercise types include muscle strengthening, aerobic, and range-of-motion
  exercise; both land-based and aquatic programs show benefit.
- Referral to a physical therapist for assessment and instruction is commonly recommended.
- The underlying trial evidence has methodological limitations (most trials at unclear
  or high risk of bias).

## Claims NOT allowed from this source

- Do not prescribe a specific exercise program, frequency, or intensity for this
  patient — that is a clinician's decision.
- Do not guarantee a specific improvement in pain or function.
- Do not present exercise as a substitute for clinician assessment.
