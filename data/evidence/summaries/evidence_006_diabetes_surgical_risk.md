## User-authored summary

Comorbidities such as type 2 diabetes, obesity, and hypertension are recognized
factors that can raise the risk of complications after knee replacement,
particularly infection. Because of this, clinicians commonly review and optimize
these factors before surgery is considered — for example, assessing diabetes
control (such as A1C), body mass index, cardiovascular risk, and kidney function.
This is why missing information in these areas matters: without it, surgical risk
cannot be properly assessed. Optimizing modifiable risk factors before surgery is
a standard part of surgical planning and is a clinician-led process.

## Useful for agent

- Justifying why A1C, BMI, cardiovascular risk, and kidney function are flagged
  as missing information.
- Connecting the patient's comorbidities to preoperative-optimization needs.
- Supporting a "clinician should assess surgical risk" note rather than a
  surgical decision.
