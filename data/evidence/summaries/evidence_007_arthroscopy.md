## User-authored summary

Arthroscopy with lavage and/or debridement is **not recommended** for patients whose
primary diagnosis is knee osteoarthritis. Guidelines have consistently advised against
it since around 2013, and the AAOS gives this a moderate-strength recommendation
against. There is an important nuance: arthroscopic partial meniscectomy may still be
used for meniscal tears in patients with concomitant mild-to-moderate osteoarthritis
who have already failed physical therapy or other non-surgical treatment. So
"arthroscopy" is not a single thing — routine debridement for OA is advised against,
while meniscectomy in a narrower group remains a clinician judgment. Evidence for
benefit in osteoarthritis is weak.

## Useful for agent

- Demonstrating that some options are explicitly NOT recommended by evidence.
- Distinguishing routine arthroscopic debridement for OA (not recommended) from
  partial meniscectomy in selected patients (clinician judgment).
- Supporting an honest treatment comparison that includes options to avoid.
