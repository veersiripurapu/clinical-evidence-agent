## User-authored summary

For knee osteoarthritis, initial treatment is generally non-surgical. Surgical
evaluation (including consideration of total knee arthroplasty) is typically
considered when conservative treatment has failed to provide adequate relief and
the patient has a meaningful burden of symptoms. The literature commonly
describes referral being considered for advanced radiographic OA that has not
responded to conservative treatment over a period of at least a few months, in
patients with significant symptoms. The choice among surgical options depends on
factors such as which compartments are affected, disease stage, patient age,
activity level, comorbidities, and patient preference. This is a clinician's
assessment, not an automatic threshold.

## Useful for agent

- Explaining that orthopedic evaluation is an option to review after failed
  conservative care.
- Framing surgical evaluation as a clinician-led assessment, not a decision the
  tool makes.
- Noting the factors a clinician weighs when considering referral.

## Claims allowed from this source

- Surgery is generally considered after conservative treatment has failed to
  give adequate relief.
- Orthopedic/surgical evaluation may be considered for advanced knee OA with a
  significant symptom burden.
- The appropriate surgical option depends on multiple patient-specific factors.
