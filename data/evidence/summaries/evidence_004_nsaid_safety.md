## User-authored summary

NSAIDs (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, such as ibuprofen) are commonly
used for osteoarthritis pain but carry well-documented risks affecting the
cardiovascular, gastrointestinal, and kidney (renal) systems. These risks are
higher in older adults and in people with diabetes, hypertension, cardiovascular
disease, or reduced kidney function. NSAID use is associated with increased risk
of acute kidney injury and gastrointestinal bleeding, and can raise blood
pressure. Guidance commonly identifies patients over 65 and those with diabetes,
hypertension, or renal impairment as higher-risk, warranting caution, lowest
effective dose, shortest duration, and clinician review. Topical NSAIDs are
generally considered lower-risk than oral forms.
