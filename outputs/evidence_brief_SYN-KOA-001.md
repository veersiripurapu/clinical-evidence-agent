### Topical NSAIDs — a lower-risk alternative to oral NSAIDs for knee osteoarthritis  
`[EVID-011]`

**Supported by this source:**
- Topical NSAIDs are a guideline-supported option for knee osteoarthritis. `[EVID-011]`
- Topical and oral NSAIDs provide broadly similar pain relief, but topical formulations are associated with fewer systemic adverse effects, including less gastrointestinal harm. `[EVID-011]`
- Topical NSAIDs are described as an appropriate consideration for older patients and those with comorbidities or risk factors for adverse effects from oral NSAIDs. `[EVID-011]`

### Conservative (non-surgical) management of knee osteoarthritis  
`[EVID-001]`

**Supported by this source:**
- Exercise is recommended over no exercise to improve pain and function in knee osteoarthritis. `[EVID-001]`
- Sustained weight loss is recommended to improve pain and function in overweight or obese patients with knee osteoarthritis. `[EVID-001]`
- Topical NSAIDs may be used to improve function and quality of life when not contraindicated. `[EVID-001]`

### When orthopedic/surgical evaluation is considered for knee osteoarthritis  
`[EVID-003]`

**Supported by this source:**
- Surgery is generally considered after conservative treatment has failed to give adequate relief. `[EVID-003]`
- Orthopedic/surgical evaluation may be considered for advanced knee OA with a significant symptom burden. `[EVID-003]`
- The appropriate surgical option depends on multiple patient-specific factors. `[EVID-003]`

### NSAID (anti-inflammatory pain medication) safety considerations  
`[EVID-004]`

**Supported by this source:**
- NSAIDs carry cardiovascular, gastrointestinal, and kidney risks. `[EVID-004]`
- These risks are higher in older adults and in people with diabetes, hypertension, or reduced kidney function. `[EVID-004]`
- Higher-risk patients warrant caution, lowest effective dose, and clinician review. `[EVID-004]`

### Risks and complications of total knee arthroplasty (knee replacement)  
`[EVID-002]`

**Supported by this source:**
- Recognized complications of knee replacement include infection, blood clots, revision surgery, and anesthesia-related risks. `[EVID-002]`
- Type 2 diabetes, obesity, and hypertension are among reported risk factors for infection after knee replacement. `[EVID-002]`
- Reported infection incidence in one analysis was about 1.55% within two years (with the source and population stated). `[EVID-002]`

### Weight loss as a treatment for knee osteoarthritis  
`[EVID-008]`

**Supported by this source:**
- Weight loss is an evidence-supported treatment for knee osteoarthritis in overweight or obese adults. `[EVID-008]`
- Benefit appears dose-dependent: greater weight loss is associated with greater improvement in pain and function. `[EVID-008]`
- Diet combined with exercise has been among the more effective approaches studied. `[EVID-008]`
